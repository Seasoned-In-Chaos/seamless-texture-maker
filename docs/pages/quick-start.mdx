# Quick Start

Follow these 6 steps to create your first seamless PBR material:

## 1. Open an Image

Press `Ctrl+O` or drag-and-drop an image onto the 2D viewport. SEAMS supports:

- PNG, JPEG, TIFF, TGA, EXR
- Minimum 64×64, maximum 8192×8192
- 8-bit, 16-bit, and 32-bit depth
- sRGB and linear color spaces

The image appears in the viewport with file info in the navigation rail's Quick Info card.

## 2. Delight (Optional)

Press `1` to switch to the Delight panel, or click the Delight icon in the navigation rail.

1. Select an **Intensity Preset**: Soft, Balanced, or Aggressive
2. Adjust sliders as needed (all default to 0 — no processing until you change them)
3. Click **APPLY DELIGHT**

<Note>
Delighting is destructive — it modifies the base image and pushes the previous state to the undo stack. You can undo with `Ctrl+Z` (up to 20 steps).
</Note>

## 3. Make Seamless

Press `2` to switch to the Seamless panel.

1. Choose a **Method**: Overlap Blend or Splat Synthesis
2. Adjust the controls for your chosen method
3. The preview updates live as you move sliders
4. After a 400ms pause, full-resolution processing runs automatically

<Tip>
Use **Overlap Blend** for architectural textures, fabrics, and any image with regular patterns. Use **Splat Synthesis** for organic materials like stone, bark, and terrain where you want to break up repetition.
</Tip>

## 4. Generate PBR Maps

Press `3` to switch to the Material Lab.

All 8 PBR maps are generated automatically from your seamless texture:

- **Normal** — Surface detail and direction
- **Roughness** — Surface smoothness
- **Metallic** — Metal vs dielectric
- **AO** — Ambient occlusion
- **Height** — Surface elevation
- **Displacement** — Tessellation displacement
- **Opacity** — Transparency mask
- **Emissive** — Self-illumination

Adjust each map's controls, then select a channel in the dock at the bottom to preview it.

## 5. Preview in 3D

Switch to **Studio Mode** via the View menu or the toggle button at the bottom of the window.

- The 3D viewport shows your PBR material on a mesh
- Change **Mesh** (Sphere, Cube, Plane) and **HDRI** lighting (Studio, Outdoor, Archviz, Neutral Gray)
- Enable **Tess** and adjust **Height** slider for displacement
- Orbit with left-click, pan with right-click, zoom with scroll wheel

## 6. Export

### Quick Export

Press `Ctrl+S` to save the current seamless texture, or `Ctrl+E` to save the selected PBR map.

### Full PBR Export

Press `Ctrl+Shift+E` to open the Production Export dialog:

1. Select your **Renderer** (UE5, Blender, V-Ray, Corona, Generic PBR)
2. Adjust format, bit depth, resolution, and map toggles
3. Set material name and output folder
4. Click **Export**

The exporter creates all map files plus renderer-specific sidecar scripts (MaxScript, Python, or UE5 material instance).
